## OLYMPIC WEIGHTLIFTING PULL PRESCRIPTION STANDARDS

### CATALYST ATHLETICS (Greg Everett):
**"Pulls should be programmed at percentages of the competition lift"**

**Standard Pull Prescriptions:**
- Snatch Pulls: 80-105% of Snatch 1RM
- Clean Pulls: 80-105% of Clean 1RM (or C&J if no separate Clean max)
- Overload Pulls: 100-110% for advanced lifters

**Typical Training Ranges:**
- Technical Work: 70-85% (focus on speed, position)
- Strength Work: 85-100% (focus on force production)
- Overload: 100-110% (advanced only, short ROM)

**Source:** "Olympic Weightlifting: A Complete Guide" (2016)

### USAW (USA Weightlifting):
**Pulls tied to competition lift percentages**

**Standard Ranges:**
- Snatch Pull: 75-105% of Snatch
- Clean Pull: 75-105% of Clean

**Programming Notes:**
- Lighter percentages (70-85%): Higher reps (3-5), speed emphasis
- Heavier percentages (85-100%): Lower reps (1-3), strength emphasis  
- Overload (100-110%): Singles, advanced lifters only

**Source:** USAW Level 1 Coach Manual (2020)

### GLENN PENDLAY / CALIFORNIASTRENGTH:
**"Pull percentages based on the lift they support"**

**Prescription Philosophy:**
- Snatch Pulls → % of Snatch
- Clean Pulls → % of Clean
- If no separate Clean max, use C&J as proxy

**Typical Ranges:**
- Accumulation: 70-85% × 3-5 reps
- Intensification: 80-95% × 2-3 reps
- Competition: 85-100% × 1-2 reps

**Source:** "The Pendlay Program" article series (2011-2015)

### TOROKHTIY (Olympic Champion):
**Pull prescriptions from Олексій Торохтій programs**

**Standard Ranges:**
- Technical Pulls: 70-85% of competition lift
- Strength Pulls: 85-100% of competition lift
- Overload: 95-105% (short ROM variations only)

**Volume Distribution:**
- Accumulation: 3-5 sets × 3-5 reps @ 70-85%
- Intensification: 3-4 sets × 2-3 reps @ 80-95%
- Realization: 2-3 sets × 1-2 reps @ 85-100%

**Source:** Torokhtiy online program archives (2018-2024)

### RUSSIAN/SOVIET METHODOLOGY:

**Medvedev, Chernyak, Vorobyev Research:**

Pull prescription philosophy:
- Pulls develop strength reserve above competition requirements
- Target: Pulls should be 5-10kg heavier than competition lifts
- This translates to 100-105% for maintenance, 105-110% for development

**Prescription Method:**
1. Determine athlete's competition lift max (Snatch or C&J)
2. For Clean Pulls, use Clean max if known, otherwise C&J max
3. Apply percentage based on training phase
4. Overload phases: 100-110% (but only for advanced lifters)

**Typical Soviet Program Ranges:**
- Preparation: 70-85% × 3-5 reps
- Competition: 80-100% × 2-3 reps
- Overload: 100-110% × 1-2 reps (advanced only)

**Source:** "A System of Multi-Year Training in Weightlifting" (1989)

### JOHN BROZ / AVERAGE BROZ'S GYM:

**"Bulgarian Method" Pull Prescription:**
- Pulls done AFTER competition lifts, NOT before
- Percentages based on DAY'S successful lift
- Typical: 105-110% of what was successfully snatched/cleaned that day
- Never programmed in advance - always reactive

**Note:** This is an OUTLIER method, not mainstream

## COMPREHENSIVE PRESCRIPTION RECOMMENDATIONS:

### SNATCH PULLS:

**Accumulation:**
- Range: 70-80% of Snatch
- Sets × Reps: 4-5 × 3-5
- Formula: `intensity + 0.05`
- Example: If Snatch @ 72%, Pulls @ 77%

**Intensification:**
- Range: 80-95% of Snatch
- Sets × Reps: 3-4 × 2-3
- Formula: `intensity + 0.10`
- Example: If Snatch @ 80%, Pulls @ 90%

**Competition:**
- Range: 85-100% of Snatch
- Sets × Reps: 2-3 × 1-2
- Formula: `intensity + 0.05` to `intensity + 0.10`
- Example: If Snatch @ 88%, Pulls @ 93-98%

### CLEAN PULLS:

**Accumulation:**
- Range: 75-85% of Clean (or C&J)
- Sets × Reps: 4-5 × 3-5
- Formula: `intensity + 0.05` to `intensity + 0.08`
- Example: If C&J @ 72%, Pulls @ 77-80%

**Intensification:**
- Range: 85-100% of Clean (or C&J)
- Sets × Reps: 3-4 × 2-3
- Formula: `intensity + 0.12` to `intensity + 0.15`
- Example: If C&J @ 80%, Pulls @ 92-95%

**Competition:**
- Range: 90-105% of Clean (or C&J)
- Sets × Reps: 2-3 × 1-2
- Formula: `intensity + 0.10` to `intensity + 0.15`
- Example: If C&J @ 88%, Pulls @ 98-103%

### RATIONALE FOR DIFFERENCES:

**Why Clean Pulls can be heavier:**
1. Clean typically more strength-limited than Snatch
2. Snatch more technique-limited
3. Clean Pulls used for overload more frequently
4. Pull-to-hip ratio is higher for Cleans

**Phase-Specific Logic:**
- **Accumulation:** Lower percentages, focus on volume and technique
- **Intensification:** Higher percentages, focus on strength development
- **Competition:** Moderate percentages, maintain strength without excessive fatigue
